FMT donor protocol

Thank you for offering to support a recipient through faecal microbiota transplant (FMT). This protocol outlines how donors are selected and the dietary and lifestyle changes you will need to make to ensure your stool is safe and viable for transplant.

01

Selection of donors

Donors are selected by the recipient on the following criteria:

  • The potential donor must have a healthy bowel motion every day.
  • No history of bowel problems (e.g. no constipation, diarrhoea, colitis etc.).
  • Is not on any medications that may interfere with stool viability, e.g. antibiotics or PPIs.

As a potential donor you will be fully screened to ensure you are free from infection. This involves a blood sample and stool tests.

02

Dietary changes

The person receiving your stool (the recipient) will be relying on you to pass a bowel motion every morning. We highly recommend that you start the following changes at least one week prior to the commencement of donating.

1. Avoiding foods at risk of contamination

  • Avoid uncooked shellfish, prawns, oysters and processed meats such as salami, ham and sausages.
  • Avoid all antibiotics.
  • No more than 1 standard drink of wine or beer 24 hours before donating.
  • No smoking.

2. Commence a high-fibre diet to improve the quality of your flora

  • All breads, cereals and grains should be wholemeal / wholegrain — including bread, pasta, rice and breakfast cereals.
  • Eat plenty of fresh vegetables.
  • Include beans and pulses in your diet (lentils, chickpeas, beans, hummus).
  • Eat at least two pieces of fruit per day.
  • Drink at least 1 L of water per day.

03

Meal suggestions

Breakfast

  • Wholemeal toast, muesli or a high-fibre cereal — perhaps include some yoghurt.

Lunch

  • Salad sandwich with wholemeal bread (any filling) and a piece of fruit.
  • Pasta with veggies.
  • Noodles with veggies.

Dinner

  • Pasta with meat, sauce and veggies.
  • Meat, fish or chicken with two types of veggies or salad and potatoes.
  • Stir-fried veggies (with or without meat) with noodles or brown rice.
  • Brown rice with beans or lentils.
04

Your responsibilities as a donor

As a donor it is vitally important that you understand the instructions provided. There are two major points:

  1. You need to make sufficient dietary and lifestyle changes for the duration of the patient's treatment to ensure that you will pass a bowel motion every day.
  2. If you experience any of the following, please advise straight away:
  • Diarrhoea
  • Vomiting
  • Cold or flu symptoms
  • Any antibiotic use
05

How to ensure you "go" every day

This is the biggest concern of the donor. By following the dietary recommendations above, you should have no problem passing a bowel motion every day.
